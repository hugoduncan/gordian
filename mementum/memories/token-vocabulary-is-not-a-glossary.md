💡 A token-based conceptual model is not enough to justify a project glossary feature. On `gordian` itself, the extracted “glossary” was dominated by root namespace tokens (`gordian`), code-shape words (`node`, `pair`, `map`), and docstring boilerplate (`return`). The useful concepts were mostly multi-word phrases like "change coupling" or "propagation cost", which the current single-token TF-IDF pipeline cannot represent. Conclusion: token vocabulary extraction may still be useful as a low-level signal, but a real glossary needs phrase-level concepts or much stronger evidence filtering. Reverting the feature was better than shipping a weak, noisy command.
